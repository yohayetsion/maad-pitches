One Address, Three Experiences

The brand unification opportunity

YOKU

Japanese Sushi & Asian Dining

Tokyo-vogue experience with Bar Tokyo cocktails. Bold, contemporary, unexpected for the Cotswolds. Currently strongest individual brand identity.

The Terrace

All-Day British Dining

Breakfast through Sunday lunch. Traditional hotel restaurant positioning. Currently the least differentiated venue — needs clearer story.

Gin & Juice

Nightlife Bar / 400+ Gins

Vibrant, lively, Cheltenham's nightlife heart. Walk-ins only. Strong personality but visually disconnected from hotel identity.

The Challenge

These three venues feel like separate businesses sharing an address. A guest booking YOKU may not realise they can stay overnight. A hotel guest might not know about the gin collection. There's no master narrative that ties them together.

Competitive Landscape

Key players in UK boutique hospitality

The Pig Hotels

"Restaurant with Rooms"

8 properties. Kitchen garden-led, 25-mile sourcing radius. Rustic-luxe aesthetic, muted earth tones. Very strong brand storytelling.

Artist Residence

Eclectic Art Hotels

5 UK locations. Design-forward, every room unique. Cream/teal palette. Appeals to creative audience seeking personality.

Soho Farmhouse

Members' Club Lifestyle

40 min from Cheltenham. Exclusive membership model, dark sophisticated aesthetic. Global brand with local character.

Country Creatures

Pubs with Rooms

Sam & Georgie Pearman (original Lucky Onion founders). Now competing in same market. Strong local credibility.

Direction 1

Unified Luxury House

"One House, Three Experiences"

Create a stronger No.131 master brand that encompasses all three venues. Each keeps its distinct personality but shares visual DNA. Position as a destination complex, not a hotel with restaurants.

Brand Architecture

No.131 as umbrella → YOKU, The Terrace, Gin & Juice as sub-brands with shared design elements

Visual Language

Consistent navy/gold palette across all venues. Shared typography system. Unified photography style.

Customer Journey

Clear pathways: "Stay + Dine at YOKU" packages. Cross-venue discovery encouraged.

Risk Level: Low

Evolution, not revolution. Builds on what exists rather than starting over.

Direction 2

The Dunkerton Collection

"Fashion, Food & Spirits — Julian & Jade's Cotswolds"

Lean fully into the founder story. Position No.131 as the hospitality expression of a lifestyle brand portfolio: Superdry's entrepreneurial spirit, Holland Cooper's British elegance, Dunkertons' craft heritage.

Brand Story

Julian met Jade at the bar here. Their personal taste shapes every detail. This is their home, and you're invited.

Cross-Brand Synergy

Holland Cooper styling throughout. Dunkertons on every menu. Superdry entrepreneurial values embedded.

Content Opportunity

"Day in the Life" content. Behind-the-scenes with Julian & Jade. Fashion meets hospitality.

Risk Level: Medium

Requires owner visibility and commitment. Very differentiated but personality-dependent.

Direction 3

Contemporary Cotswolds

"Georgian Meets Tokyo"

Embrace the unexpected. Position No.131 as the Cotswolds' most eclectic luxury hotel. Lead with YOKU's distinctive Japanese concept. Challenge the "cream teas and tweed" stereotype while respecting the architecture.

Positioning

Not your grandmother's Cotswolds. Traditional architecture, contemporary experiences.

Target Audience

Younger, more adventurous. London weekenders seeking something different. Design-conscious travelers.

Visual Direction

Bolder contrasts. Japanese influence in graphic design. More editorial photography style.

Risk Level: Higher

More disruptive positioning. Could alienate traditional Cotswolds audience but strongly differentiates from competitors.

Our Recommendation

Direction 1 + Direction 2

Unified Luxury House with Strong Founder Story

Create the master brand architecture of Direction 1 (one house, three experiences) while weaving in the founder narrative of Direction 2. Julian and Jade's story is unique — Superdry meets Holland Cooper meets Georgian hospitality. No other Cotswolds hotel has this.

This gives you differentiation from The Pig (no kitchen garden to copy) and Soho House (not exclusive members' club) while building on genuine assets you already own.
