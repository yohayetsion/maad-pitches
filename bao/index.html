Competitive Landscape

Key players in London's Asian casual dining scene

Flesh & Buns

"Japanese Izakaya ‚Äî DIY buns"

Bone Daddies group. Rock 'n' roll energy. Build-your-own hirata buns. Dark, industrial aesthetic. Japanese, not Taiwanese.

Koya

"Fast food with class"

Handmade udon noodles. Queue-worthy authenticity. Cheap, cheerful, no-frills. Minimal design. Japanese, food-first.

Various Ramen

Kanada-Ya, Shoryu, Ippudo

Ramen-focused. Range from authentic (Kanada-Ya) to chain (Shoryu). Compete for same "quick Asian" occasions.

BAO's Advantage: No direct competitor combines Taiwanese identity + design-led experience + multi-concept portfolio. The threat isn't specific competitors ‚Äî it's other design-forward, photo-worthy Asian concepts competing for the same audience.

Direction 1

Design Purist

"A gallery that serves incredible food"

Lean harder into the art school origins. Make every touchpoint feel like visiting an exhibition. Elevate the Wes Anderson/Issey Miyake influences. This approach deepens your moat against competitors who can copy food but not design DNA.

Visual Approach

More white space. Elevated grid systems. Gallery-style photography. The Lonely Man as contemporary art icon, not just mascot.

Palette

Pure black/white. Restraint as statement. Color only from food.

Typography

Tighter grid. Precise spacing. Typography as form. Consider custom display face that references Taiwanese signage.

Website

Museum-quality presentation. Each restaurant as an exhibition. Slower pacing, more considered navigation.

Direction 2

Taiwanese Culture Ambassador

"A bridge between Taipei and London"

Deepen the Taiwan connection. Surface the Taipei pop-ups, regional influences, and cultural bridges. This positions BAO not just as "Taiwanese food in London" but as a genuine cultural exchange ‚Äî the authentic voice of Taiwanese food culture in the UK.

Visual Approach

More Taiwanese visual references. Night market energy. Neon inspiration from Taipei signage. Warmer, more vibrant.

Palette

Black base. Red and gold accents. Lucky colours. Night market warmth.

Storytelling

Erchen's Taipei roots prominent. "From Muzha to Soho" narrative. Taiwan pop-up content integrated, not hidden.

Website

Taiwan section/microsite. Regional food stories. More cultural context around dishes. Education + entertainment.

Direction 3

Empire Builder

"From Hackney car park to seven restaurants"

Celebrate the journey. The scrappy-to-sophisticated narrative is compelling but undersold. Three art students, one stall, now a Michelin-recognised empire with karaoke rooms and a Phaidon cookbook. This is founder storytelling at its best.

Visual Approach

Timeline/journey motif. Then vs. now imagery. Behind-the-scenes authenticity. Founders more visible.

Palette

Black/white core. Warm gold for growth/achievement moments. Documentary aesthetic.

Founder Content

Erchen, Shing, Wai Ting more prominent. Art school background celebrated. "Built by artists" positioning.

Website

Story-first homepage. Timeline of growth. Milestones celebrated. Future vision teased.

Our Recommendation

Direction 1 + 3: Design Purist Meets Growth Story

Elevate the aesthetic. Amplify the journey.

BAO's design DNA is its moat. No competitor can replicate the art school credibility, the Wes Anderson references, the Lonely Man. Double down on this ‚Äî make the website feel like an exhibition.

But also tell the growth story. The journey from Hackney car park to seven restaurants is inspiring and undersold. Combine these: world-class design that celebrates an authentic founder journey.
