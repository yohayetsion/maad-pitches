Assessment

Strengths & Opportunities

What's Working Well

  • Distinctive positioning - Kitchen garden philosophy is ownable and genuine
  • Exceptional photography - Lifestyle shots feel authentic, not staged
  • Consistent voice - Warm copy across all touchpoints
  • Social proof - Active #thepighotel community
  • Sustainability credentials - Three-star Food Made Good rating

Room to Grow

  • Booking experience - Doesn't quite capture on-property warmth
  • Mobile optimization - Image-rich approach needs refinement
  • Room comparison - 9 properties x room types = complexity
  • Color palette - Could expand with seasonal garden tones
  • Menu storytelling - 25-mile philosophy could come alive digitally

Market Context

Competitive Landscape

The Pig occupies a unique position - more accessible than exclusive clubs, more characterful than traditional country houses.

Lime Wood
Sister brand - 5* luxury

Angela Hartnett collaboration, Herb House Spa. Higher price point, more refined positioning. Same ownership, complementary not competitive.

Soho Farmhouse
Members' club exclusivity

100 acres, celebrity crowd, from 325/month membership. The Pig offers similar escape without barriers - more democratic luxury.

Artist Residence
Bohemian boutique

Design-forward, urban locations, lower price point. Appeals to creative urbanites. Less food focus, more aesthetics.

Calcot Collection
Family-friendly Cotswolds

Strong spa and kids' facilities. More traditional country house positioning. Competes directly with PIG-at Combe.

1
Conservative Evolution
Kitchen Garden Evolution

Refine without reinventing. Keep the warm foundation, enhance the digital experience with thoughtful improvements.

Visual Enhancements

  • Expand color palette with seasonal garden tones
  • Introduce subtle texture patterns (linen, soil, leaves)
  • Refine typography hierarchy for better readability
  • Custom illustrated icons for room amenities

UX Improvements

  • Warmer booking flow with garden imagery
  • Property comparison tool
  • Interactive 25-mile map showing suppliers
  • Seasonal menu preview in booking journey

Best For

Teams who want to honor what Robin and Judy built while creating a more immersive digital experience. Low risk, high polish.

2
Bolder Approach
Rustic Luxury Amplified

Push the brand forward while staying rooted. More confident, more distinctive, more memorable.

Brand Evolution

  • Commission custom display typeface
  • Bespoke illustration system (not stock)
  • Motion design for key moments
  • Richer, more confident color palette

Experience Innovation

  • Virtual kitchen garden walk-through
  • Meet the team video introductions
  • "Design your stay" interactive tool
  • Real-time what's growing today feature

Best For

Teams ready to differentiate more boldly from competitors. Creates distance from traditional country house hotels.

3
Story-First Approach
The 25-Mile Experience

Lead with the story that makes you unique. The 25-mile philosophy becomes the organizing principle for the entire digital experience.

Content Strategy

  • Interactive map as navigation paradigm
  • Supplier stories at the heart of booking
  • Seasonal content architecture
  • Chef and gardener video diaries

Platform Features

  • "What's on the menu this week" dynamic content
  • Weather-responsive imagery and messaging
  • Booking tied to seasonal experiences
  • Post-stay recipe sharing

Best For

Teams who see the 25-mile philosophy as the ultimate differentiator and want to build the entire brand experience around it.

Our View

Recommendation

We recommend starting with Direction 1 (Kitchen Garden Evolution) with selected elements from Direction 3 (The 25-Mile Experience).

Why This Approach

  • Honors the strong brand foundation you've built
  • Low risk - evolution not revolution
  • Story-first elements increase differentiation
  • Can be implemented incrementally
  • Measurable impact on booking conversion

Priority Focus Areas

  • Phase 1: Booking flow warmth + mobile optimization
  • Phase 2: Property comparison tool + seasonal content
  • Phase 3: Interactive 25-mile map + supplier stories

Immediate Impact

Quick Wins

High-impact improvements that can be implemented quickly without a full rebrand.

1

Booking Flow Warmth

Add garden imagery and warm copy to the booking engine. Transform transactional screens into brand moments. "Choose your room" becomes "Find your perfect snug."

2

Mobile Image Optimization

Audit and optimize all imagery for mobile. Consider art direction - some images need recropping for portrait format, not just resizing.

3

Property Quick-Compare

Simple side-by-side tool: "Which PIG is right for you?" Location, room count, signature features, price range. Reduce choice paralysis.

4

Today's Garden Feature

Dynamic homepage element showing what's being harvested today. Real content, updated regularly by kitchen garden team. Proves the philosophy is real.
