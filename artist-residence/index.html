Direction 1

The Founders' Collection

"From a Gumtree ad to MICHELIN Keys"

Lead with your story

No other boutique hotel group has an origin story like yours. Justin's mother's accident, dropping out of university, the Gumtree ad inviting artists, Charlotte renovating Penzance with no experience — this is the most compelling narrative in UK hospitality. Make it visible.

Positioning

Personal, warm, authentic. Each hotel as a chapter in the Salisbury journey. Counter to corporate hotel chains.

Palette

Voice

Personal storytelling. "We" not "The brand." Inviting guests into your world, not just your hotels.

Risk Level

Low — Builds on existing authenticity. Evolution, not revolution.

Direction 2

Art in Residence

"Stay where art lives"

Reclaim the artistic positioning

You're called "Artist Residence" but the art story is invisible. Feature artist collaborations, create an art trail across properties, commission new works. Position as cultural destinations, not just hotels. Take the name literally.

Positioning

Gallery meets hotel. Art is the heartbeat. Each property curated by local artists. Cultural destination.

Palette

Voice

Creative, curatorial. Artist profiles, commission stories. "Meet the makers" content.

Risk Level

Medium — Requires ongoing artist partnerships and content investment.

Direction 3

The Character Collection

"Five hotels. Five stories. One soul."

Celebrate each property's uniqueness

Brighton is bohemian and coastal. London is Pimlico pop art. Oxfordshire is steampunk country. Cornwall is Georgian seaside. Bristol is industrial heritage. Make each a distinct experience under one umbrella.

Positioning

Character over chain. Each property a destination. Unified by soul, not sameness.

Palette

Voice

Location-specific storytelling. "In Brighton, we..." vs "In Oxfordshire, we..." Regional pride.

Risk Level

Medium — Complex execution, potential brand dilution if not unified.

Our Recommendation

Direction 1: The Founders' Collection

With elements borrowed from Direction 2

Your origin story is unlike anything in UK hospitality. From a Gumtree ad for artists to MICHELIN Keys at all five properties — that's not marketing, it's genuine. And it's completely invisible on your website.

Lead with the Salisbury story. Let guests feel they're entering your world, not just booking a room. Keep the art narrative alive (it's in your name) but ground it in the personal journey that made it all possible.

This differentiates from The Pig (food-led), Lime Wood (spa-led), and Soho House (members-led). Your lead is founders. The accidental hoteliers who created something real.
