Key Messaging

Visual Style

Dark colour scheme with gold accents. Playful, medium energy. Monkey mascot throughout. Food-forward photography.

Target Customer

  • Urban professionals (25-45)
  • Seeking authentic Indian beyond curry houses
  • Values quality, provenance, ethical business
  • Willing to pay premium for experience

Market Position

  • Between Dishoom (premium heritage) and Bundobust (casual beer)
  • White space: Own "Indian home cooking" narrative
  • 25+ locations across UK — strong expansion

Current State Analysis

What's working and where there's room to grow

Strengths

  • Compelling founder story (Nisha's journey from barrister)
  • Clear positioning vs. traditional curry houses
  • Consistent dark palette with gold accents
  • Strong community focus (Mowgli Trust — £1M+ raised)
  • Media presence & credibility (MBE, BBC, 5 cookbooks)
  • Rapid expansion — 25+ locations

Opportunities

  • Founder story is buried — could be more prominent
  • Homepage lacks clear visual hierarchy
  • Hero section is promotional, not brand-building
  • Mobile navigation could be simplified
  • Differentiation from Dishoom could be sharper
  • Photography consistency varies across pages

Competitive Landscape

Key players in UK Indian casual dining

Dishoom

"From Bombay with Love"

11 cafés. Bombay heritage nostalgia, literary sophistication, premium pricing. Cream/charcoal palette, serif typography. Very strong brand storytelling.

Bundobust

"Indian Street Food & Craft Beer"

Multiple UK cities. Playful, high energy, younger audience. Yellow/burgundy palette. Key differentiator: beer pairing.

Masala Zone

Regional Indian cuisine

Family-friendly, accessible pricing. More traditional positioning. Broader menu appeal.

Direction 1

Refined Evolution

"The same Mowgli, elevated"

Keep the dark palette and warmth that customers know, but modernize typography, strengthen storytelling, and create more visual breathing room. This is the lowest-risk path — evolution, not revolution.

Palette

Typography

Refined custom display + modern sans-serif. Cleaner hierarchy.

Direction 2

Heritage Reinvented

"Contemporary Indian, not colonial nostalgia"

Take inspiration from modern Indian design — vibrant colours, geometric patterns, bold typography. This positions Mowgli as forward-looking Indian, distinct from Dishoom's vintage Bombay aesthetic.

Palette

Typography

Bold geometric display + clean sans-serif. High contrast, modern energy.

Direction 3

Premium Home Kitchen

"Your neighbourhood Indian home kitchen"

Lean fully into the "home cooking" positioning. Warmer, more inviting, less restaurant-y. Earth tones, hand-crafted feel, artisanal authenticity. Makes Mowgli feel like being invited into someone's home.

Palette

Typography

Warm serif headlines + friendly sans body. Approachable, not formal.

Our Recommendation

Direction 1: Refined Evolution

With elements borrowed from Direction 3

Mowgli has strong brand recognition. A full rebrand risks confusing loyal customers. Instead, we recommend evolving the current identity — better storytelling, cleaner hierarchy, and warmer touches that reinforce the "home cooking" message.

This gives you differentiation from Dishoom (warmer, more personal) while keeping the sophisticated dark palette that works across your 25+ locations.
