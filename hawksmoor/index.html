Current State Analysis

What's working and where there's room to grow

Strengths

  • Genuine premium position — "the best steak" (Times, GQ)
  • Clear British beef differentiation vs. Argentine/US competitors
  • Compelling founder story (Will & Huw, friends since age 11)
  • Strong sustainability credentials (B Corp, carbon neutral)
  • Rare Breeds programme — unique provenance story
  • Multi-location scale without losing quality perception

Opportunities

  • Hero section is promotional — brand story is buried
  • Founder story not visible on homepage
  • Multiple image carousels create visual noise
  • Dense navigation with competing CTAs
  • Typography inconsistency (mixed font families)
  • Sustainability story deserves higher prominence
  • Shop feels separate from main brand experience
  • Mobile navigation could be simplified

Competitive Landscape

Key players in UK premium steakhouse market

Gaucho

"The best Argentinian restaurant"

20+ UK locations. Argentine Pampas beef, extensive Malbec list. Dark, moody interiors with leather accents. International glamour positioning.

Goodman

"Great steak, great wine, great people"

3 London locations (Mayfair, City, Canary Wharf). On-site dry aging rooms. Multi-origin beef. "Clubhouse" feel for business dining.

CUT at 45 Park Lane

Wolfgang Puck's European debut

Single ultra-premium location. Dorchester Collection hotel. Widest cut selection (Wagyu, USDA Prime). Damien Hirst artwork, celebrity chef brand.

M Restaurant

"International steaks from 6 countries"

2 London locations. Global beef sourcing (USA, Argentina, Japan, France, South Africa, Australia). High-shine, matt black aesthetics.

Hawksmoor's differentiator: British beef provenance + sustainability story + founder authenticity

Direction 1

Modern British Steakhouse

"The British Steakhouse"

Refresh the visual identity with a more contemporary, confident British aesthetic — clean, bold, unapologetically premium. Homepage restructure puts brand story first, promotions second. This is evolution that strengthens without disruption.

Risk Level

Medium — Requires design refresh but maintains brand equity and recognition.

Direction 2

British Stewardship

"Guardians of British Beef"

Double down on the British farming and sustainability story. Position Hawksmoor as the guardian of British beef heritage — from Rare Breeds programme to carbon neutrality. More prominent founder story, provenance photography showing farms and butchers.

Direction 3

The Hawksmoor Experience

"Every Meal Worth Remembering"

Shift focus from product (steak) to experience (hospitality, atmosphere, people). Lean into the award-winning position and human stories. More lifestyle imagery — celebrations, staff profiles, location-specific storytelling. Emotional connection over product features.

Risk Level

Our Recommendation

Direction 1: Modern British Steakhouse

With elements from Direction 2 (British heritage/sustainability story)

Hawksmoor has built something rare — genuine premium positioning backed by substance. A full rebrand would be unnecessary and risky. Instead, we recommend refining the digital experience to match the in-person excellence.

This means: homepage restructure with brand story first, typography cleanup, stronger sustainability narrative, and the founder story visible where it matters. Your British beef differentiation is your moat against Gaucho and CUT — it deserves a bigger digital stage.

The result: cleaner, more confident, unmistakably British.
